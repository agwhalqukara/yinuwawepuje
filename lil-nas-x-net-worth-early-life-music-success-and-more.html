    Who is Lil Nas X?

    Early life

    Born on April 9, 1999, in Lithia Springs, Georgia, Montero Lamar Hill’s parents divorced when he was six.

    He initially lived with his mother and grandmother in Atlanta’s Bankhead Courts housing project but later moved in with his gospel singer father in Austell.

    He began using the Internet at 13, coinciding with the rise of memes as entertainment.

    His teenage years were marked by solitude, and he turned to Twitter to create witty memes.

    Montero struggled with his homosexuality during this time but eventually accepted it around the age of 16 or 17.

    He briefly played trumpet but quit due to fear of seeming uncool.

    He attended Lithia Springs High School and graduated in 2017.

    Later, he enrolled at the University of West Georgia, majoring in computer science, but dropped out after a year to pursue music.

    During this period, he supported himself with jobs at Zaxby’s restaurants and Six Flags Over Georgia.

    In September 2019, he surprised his high school with a performance.

    What is Lil Nas X’s Net Worth?

    Lil Nas X, the American rapper, singer, and songwriter, possesses a net worth of $9 million.

    His fame soared with the unique fusion of rap and country in “Old Town Road.”

    While TikTok sparked its rise, it ultimately earned diamond certification in 2019.

    Lil Nas X is also celebrated for his prominent role within the LGBTQ community.

    Following the release of “Old Town Road,” he openly revealed his homosexuality, marking a historic moment as the first artist with a number-one record to do so.

    His announcement garnered diverse reactions, with fans and fellow artists congratulating him, while certain members of the hip-hop community expressed criticism.

    The hip-hop world has seen very few artists openly embrace their gay identity.

    Nas X’s association with “Old Town Road” led to numerous accolades.

    Notably, he emerged as the most-nominated male artist at the 2020 Grammys, securing a Grammy for Best Music Video and Best Pop Duo Performance.

    Additionally, Nas X clinched two MTV Video Music Awards and even a Country Music Award, establishing himself as the sole LGBTQ individual to win the latter.

    Today, Lil Nas X is widely recognized as one of the internet’s most influential figures.

    Music Success

    In 2019, a shift towards a music career occurred.

    Lil Nas X began songwriting and recording, adopting the name as a tribute to New York rapper Nas.

    In 2018, he acquired the beat for “Old Town Road” from Dutch producer YoungKio for $30.

    YoungKio posted the track on a lucrative online store for producers, capitalizing on Lil Nas X’s success.

    “Old Town Road” was initially recorded at a minor Atlanta studio during “$20 Tuesdays.”

    TikTok users discovered it, launching the #Yeehaw Challenge, transforming this simple song about riding into a viral sensation.

    The track performed well on the Billboard Hot 100 and Hot Country Songs Chart before being controversially removed for “not being country enough.”

    This sparked debates about race, although Billboard claimed it was unrelated.

    Multiple remixes ensued, including one featuring Billy Ray Cyrus.

    After seven consecutive weeks atop the Billboard charts, Lil Nas X surprised Cyrus with a new Maserati, sharing it on social media.

    “Old Town Road” eventually spent a record-breaking 19 weeks at the chart’s peak.

    In September 2021, “Montero,” Lil Nas X’s debut studio album, dropped, featuring hit singles like “Montero (Call Me by Your Name)” and “Industry Baby.
